# rackmyself.com — Project Instructions

## Prices in Articles

**Always search for current prices before including any price in an article.** Do not use prices from training data or previous conversations — they go stale quickly.

- Use WebSearch to look up the current Amazon price for any product before writing or editing a price
- Write prices as approximations (e.g., "~$170") since they fluctuate
- If a product's price is unusually volatile (RAM, GPUs), note that in the article
- Amazon affiliate links use the tag `YOURTAG-20` as a placeholder — the user will replace this with their real Associates tag when they sign up

## Content Standards

- Articles should be 1,200+ words, beginner-friendly, and SEO-optimized
- Lead with a clear recommendation — don't bury the answer
- Include comparison tables where multiple products are compared
- Include a Troubleshooting section and an FAQ section in every guide
- Cross-link to other articles on the site where relevant
- Do not include speculative or unverified technical claims — search to verify benchmarks and specs

## Amazon Links

- Use direct ASIN links (`https://www.amazon.com/dp/ASIN?tag=YOURTAG-20`) for stable products (SSDs, UPS, switches)
- Use search links (`https://www.amazon.com/s?k=search+terms&tag=YOURTAG-20`) for volatile products like mini PCs and GPUs where specific listings go out of stock frequently
